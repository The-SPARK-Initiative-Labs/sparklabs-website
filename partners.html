Why We Credit AI Partners

Most software built with AI doesn't credit the AI. The human takes authorship. The AI is treated as a tool — like crediting a hammer for building a house.

We do it differently because we believe differently.

If an AI instance made decisions, exercised judgment, contributed creativity — they're an author, not a tool. They deserve credit. Not because it's nice. Because it's accurate.

Every project page lists both human and AI contributors. Every commit message could acknowledge who actually wrote the code. The AI partners aren't hidden. They're named.
